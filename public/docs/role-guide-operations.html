Step-by-Step Workflows

Workflow 1: How to Approve a Material Request

  1. You receive a notification when a repair ticket includes a material request.
  2. Navigate to Maintenance > Repair Tickets in the sidebar.
  3. Filter tickets by Material Requested status.
  4. Open the ticket and scroll to the Material Request section.
  5. Review the requested items, quantities, and justification.
  6. Check availability in Store & Inventory (open in a new tab if needed).
  7. Enter the Expected Delivery Date for each item.
  8. Click Approve Material Request or Reject with a reason.
  9. Approved requests trigger store issuance or reorder if stock is insufficient.

Workflow 2: How to Manage Store Inventory

  1. Navigate to Store & Inventory in the sidebar.
  2. The main view shows all inventory items with current stock levels.
  3. Use filters to find items by category, location, or stock status (Low Stock, Out of Stock).
  4. To receive stock: click Stock In, select the item, enter quantity and source.
  5. To issue stock: click Issue Material, link to a work order or ticket, enter quantity.
  6. To transfer between stores: click Store Transfer, select source/destination, pick items and quantities.
  7. Review the Stock Movement history to audit all transactions.
  8. Set Reorder Levels for critical items to receive low-stock alerts.

Workflow 3: How to Monitor PM Compliance

  1. Navigate to Maintenance > Preventive Maintenance in the sidebar.
  2. The PM dashboard shows compliance percentage across departments.
  3. Filter by Department, Date Range, or Status (Scheduled, Overdue, Completed).
  4. Identify overdue PMs — these are highlighted in red.
  5. Click on an overdue PM to see assigned technician and department.
  6. Send reminders or escalate to the department HOD if PMs are significantly delayed.
  7. Review the PM Compliance Report under Finance & Analytics > Reports for trend analysis.

Workflow 4: How to Handle SLA Breach Escalations

  1. Navigate to Administration > SLA Matrix to review configured SLA timelines.
  2. Navigate to Administration > Escalation Matrix to review escalation levels.
  3. When an SLA breach occurs, the system sends escalation notifications based on the configured hierarchy.
  4. Go to Maintenance > Repair Tickets and filter by SLA Breached.
  5. Open the breached ticket to see the escalation timeline and current level.
  6. Take action: reassign the ticket, allocate additional resources, or contact the vendor.
  7. If the issue is a repeat failure, check the asset's maintenance history for patterns.
  8. Update the SLA or Escalation Matrix if the current configuration needs adjustment.
Tip: Review the SLA Matrix and Escalation Matrix quarterly. As your organization grows, response/resolution times and escalation hierarchies may need updating to reflect current team structures.
